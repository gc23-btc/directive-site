Why it exists

Most agent software either over-romanticizes autonomy or reduces you to approvals everywhere.

Directive is the middle path: a thin harness, strong local skills, explicit approval boundaries, and a control surface that still feels like it belongs to one person.

It is not a simulated company. It is not boardroom cosplay. It is your desk, your memory, your agents, and your authority.

Guardian

The approval boundary is explicit, not hand-wavy.

G0

Observe and prepare only.

G1

Local, reversible, auto-executable with verification.

G2

Delegated authority with stronger packet evidence.

G3

Owner pause before consequential external motion.

G4

Principal-only approval for irreversible or constitutional change.

Directive keeps escalation legible without turning every action into bureaucracy. The system is biased toward momentum on reversible work and visible pause on irreversible work.

That means less silent overreach, less manual paralysis, and cleaner recovery when an action crosses the wrong line.
